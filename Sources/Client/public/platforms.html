Swift Without Borders

Swift has grown far beyond iOS. Today it compiles to native binaries on Apple platforms, Linux servers, WebAssembly for browsers, and even bare-metal microcontrollers. I embrace this versatility, using Swift as a single language across every layer of the stack.

Shared Code Philosophy

The real power of multi-platform Swift is code reuse. Business logic, data models, validation rules, and networking layers can be shared across iOS, macOS, server, and Wasm targets — reducing duplication and ensuring consistency everywhere.
